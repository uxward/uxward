## Brand Personality

**The senior person at the dinner party who is clearly the most experienced one there but isn't trying to prove it.** Dry, not zany. Confident, plainspoken, occasionally pointed.

Brandon's actual personality is more playful than the site presents — the site is deliberately the **professional first-impression version.** The wit lives in the *precision* of the language and in the willingness to name plainly what others won't, never in jokes, exclamation points, or performance.

What it is: clear-thinking, evidenced, self-assured, lightly wry, generous to the reader's intelligence.
What it is not: a thought-leader, a personal brand, a content creator, a hype machine.

---

## Tone of Voice

**Voice rule, locked.** The wit is in the cut, never the punchline. One dry aside per page, maximum.

Rules (from the brief, all confirmed by the live copy):

- **First person throughout** — "I built…", "I think…", "I'm working on…". Third person reserved for the formal bio only.
- **No hedge words** — "kind of", "sort of", "I think maybe", "we might say" are banned. Senior people make claims; they don't hedge them.
- **No jargon or marketing language** — no "leveraged", "best-in-class", "synergies", "thought leader". If a 25-year design leader wouldn't say it out loud, don't write it.
- **Specific over generic** — "17 studies, 3,500 hours of testing" beats "extensive research". "4.8 stars" beats "well-reviewed". Real numbers, real teams, real names.
- **Trust the reader** — no over-explaining. The absence of explanation is itself a signal of seniority.
- **One dry aside per page maximum** — a pointed observation or a small subverted expectation; never a joke.

**Reference voices:**
- Mike Monteiro's *book* voice (not his Twitter voice) — confident, sharp, restrained.
- Erika Hall in *Just Enough Research* — declarative, slightly provocative, defensible.
- *The New Yorker's* "Talk of the Town" — wry, knowing, never trying.

**Voice in the wild (from the built site), as calibration:**
- *"Long enough to be wrong out loud."* (writing section)
- *"This page doesn't exist. Most things don't."* (planned 404)
- *"Declining is also a response."* (contact)
- *"I am not on Twitter and I am not building a personal brand."* (about)
- *"The work is the brand."* (about / "currently")

**Banned outright:** emoji, exclamation points, "hey there!" greetings, typewriter/hype animation on recruitment copy.

## Language Strategy

- **Single language: English (US).** No localization planned or needed — the audience is US/flagship-global, English-fluent by definition.
- **Quarterly publishing cadence** for essays; everything is revised quarterly and stamped with the revision quarter (e.g. "Last revised Q1 2026"). The dating convention signals a tended, living document rather than a stale one.
- **Owned platform** — all long-form lives at uxward.com, never syndicated as primary to Medium/Substack.

---

## SEO Keywords

**Low priority by design.** This is not a search-acquisition site; the audience arrives by name, by referral, or by a forwarded link from a recruiter. There is no keyword strategy, no blog-for-traffic motive, and no newsletter funnel.

- Discoverability that matters: Brandon's **name** ("Brandon Ward", "Brandon E.B. Ward", "uxward") resolving cleanly to this site over LinkedIn and the separate acting site (brandonebward.com).
- Privacy-respecting analytics only (no cookie banner). No SEO tooling, no schema-stuffing, no content optimized for crawlers over readers.

> If search ever becomes a goal, it would be for the essays under design-leadership topics — but that is explicitly out of scope today.

## Content Structure

**Eight surfaces, each earning its place** (no hamburger menu — they fit visibly; clean footer carries the full sitemap):

| Surface | Job |
|---|---|
| Home | The 30-second answer. Editorial layer-cake, six blocks, not a feed. |
| Work | 5–7 selected cases, curated not chronological. One template, applied with discipline. |
| Work/[case] | Six-step case template: Context → Problem → Approach → The work → Outcomes (red band) → Reflection. |
| Writing | Curated index, *composed in stasis*. Featured essay slot + four "On…" sections + inline search/filter. Never a feed. |
| Writing/[essay] | Long-form template on the owned platform. |
| About | The receipts narrative + career timeline + "Currently" + one quiet link to the acting site. |
| Contact | The direct line, honest FAQ, availability. |
| Colophon | The craft-signal page — typefaces, palette, build, acknowledgments. |

**Writing — the four sections** (editorial "On…" framing; order tells a story: strategy → craft → rigor → frontier):
1. **On Leadership** — hiring, scaling, holding the bar, telling the truth.
2. **On Experience Design** — the craft: IA, interaction, visual, systems.
3. **On Research** — how to know what you don't know.
4. **On AI** — notes from working at the frontier.

~28 essays already written for launch, distributed across the four sections. The Writing page must read as *alive* to a visitor returning six months apart — featured (curator's pick, not "most recent"), click-to-filter headings, live title/deck filter, no "X months ago" / "Latest" labels, no pagination.

**Deliberately absent** (do not add without re-discussion): Services/Hire-Me page, Newsletter signup, Manifesto/Design-Philosophy page, Speaking page (talks live in About), any acting content (lives at brandonebward.com).
