# Tessa the Talent Partner — Persona 03

> Priority: Secondary
> Business goals served: G3 (Be sought, not seeking) · G1 (Land the right role) · G2 (Make the case undeniable)

---

## Who Tessa Is

Tessa is an executive recruiter or in-house talent lead sourcing senior design leadership — either retained by a flagship/legacy company or hunting on its behalf. She works a pipeline and a reputation at the same time: every candidate she forwards is a small bet on her own judgment in front of a hiring VP like Vivian. She is the *amplifier* in the system — she finds Brandon, qualifies him in minutes, and forwards a link that has to make *her* look smart.

---

## Psychological Profile

Tessa runs on **volume and speed**. She is juggling many requisitions, so she qualifies fast and moves on; a candidate has minutes, not a careful read. She is **less of a design expert than Vivian** and knows it — she can't fully evaluate craft, so she leans on **legible, packageable proof points**: clear titles, recognizable company names, "live product," real numbers, and signals she can repeat with confidence. Her core skill is **packaging and pitching** — turning a candidate into a clean, credible story a hiring leader will say yes to meeting.

Her currency is **presenting credible candidates**. A good forward builds her standing with clients; a bad one erodes it. So she is constantly, half-consciously asking two things: *Can I substantiate this?* and *Will this person make me look smart or make me look careless?* She is also drawn to candidates who are **easy to work with** — clear about what they want, realistic, responsive — because a messy candidate makes a messy process that reflects on her.

---

## Internal State

Tessa feels **brisk, transactional, and reputation-conscious**. There's none of Vivian's existential weight per glance — but there is a steady, low-grade **risk-awareness**: she does not want to be the one who forwarded the flameout. When a candidate is instantly packageable and obviously verifiable, she feels **relief and momentum** — *this one's easy, I can move it today.* When the picture is vague or high-maintenance, she feels mild **friction** and quietly deprioritizes.

---

## Usage Context

**How she finds the product:** Active sourcing — LinkedIn, referrals, a name that surfaces from a talk or article — then a click to uxward.com to qualify. Or a colleague passes her the link.
**Emotional state on arrival:** Brisk and evaluative, scanning for whether you're worth the effort of a forward.
**Behavior pattern:** Fast triage. Grabs the headline proof points, checks what you're looking for, and decides in minutes whether to package and pitch.
**Decision criteria:** Packageable proof (title, named companies, live product, numbers) → clarity on the role/level/terms you want → verifiability she can stand behind → easy-to-share assets. Vagueness, an unclear ask, or unverifiable claims send her to the next candidate.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **G3 — Be sought, not seeking:** Tessa is the literal mechanism of "sought" — she's the human who carries Brandon into rooms he isn't in. The clearer and more packageable the site, the more she forwards.
- ✅ **G1 — Land the right role:** She controls a major top-of-funnel path to the role; a clean read on Brandon's desired role/level/terms routes him to the *right* reqs, not just any.
- ✅ **G2 — Make the case undeniable:** She needs the same proof Vivian does, but *verifiable and quotable* — the case must be undeniable to a non-expert who has to repeat it secondhand.
