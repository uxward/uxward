# Vivian the VP of Design — Persona 02

> Priority: Primary
> Business goals served: G1 (Land the right role) · G2 (Make the case undeniable) · G3 (Be sought, not seeking)

---

## Who Vivian Is

Vivian runs design at a company where the discipline has earned a real seat — either a flagship consumer/streaming brand (LEGO, Netflix, Hulu) or a large legacy industrial group transforming itself (Caterpillar, John Deere). She owns an open senior-leadership requisition and a stack of candidates who all look strong on paper. She is not HR; she is the design leader Brandon would report to or stand beside, and she is the hardest, most discerning reader the site will ever get. She has hired at this level before — and been burned before.

---

## Psychological Profile

Vivian values **clarity of thought, specificity, and shipped proof** above all else. Years of reviewing polished-but-empty portfolios have taught her that surface gloss hides hollowness, so she reads **fast and skeptically**, hunting for the one tell that a candidate actually *thinks* and actually *builds*. Adjectives don't move her; a live product with real numbers does. She is fluent in the difference between someone who narrates work and someone who did it.

Her emphasis shifts by arena. In the **consumer/streaming** context she weights **craft, taste, and customer delight** most heavily. In the **legacy-industrial** context she weights **transformation, standing-up-practice-from-scratch, and bottom-line range** — proof a leader can drop into an unfamiliar vertical and still deliver. In both, the bar is the same: **specificity and evidence**, and a leader who **raises the bar without needing to be managed.**

She also trusts **social proof**. Talks, articles, case studies, and — crucially — recognizing names, work, or connections she already respects make her feel she is not taking a flier. A hire she can place in a network she trusts is a hire that protects her.

---

## Internal State

When Vivian evaluates a peer-level design leader, she feels **time-pressed, skeptical, and quietly exposed** — every senior hire puts her own credibility and a year of her roadmap on the line. Underneath the scanning is a hopeful, almost weary wish to be **convinced**: she *wants* to find the rare candidate who is obviously the real thing so she can stop searching. The fastest way to lose her is to make her **work for the point** or to trip her **buzzword alarm**; the fastest way to win her is to make her think *"oh — this one actually ships."*

---

## Usage Context

**How she finds the product:** A recruiter or talent partner forwards the link; a mutual connection mentions Brandon; or she searches his name after seeing a talk, article, or his work. Rarely cold — usually with a small prior signal.
**Emotional state on arrival:** Skeptical and time-poor, braced to be unimpressed, scanning for the disqualifying tell.
**Behavior pattern:** Scans, does not read paragraphs at first. Reads headlines and decks, checks recency, and looks for one concrete proof point before deciding whether to slow down and actually read the hero case or an essay.
**Decision criteria:** Shipped proof (live product, real numbers) → evidence of clear thinking / a specific POV → range across verticals → recency of hands-on work → social proof. Any whiff of vagueness, marketing language, or friction and she closes the tab.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **G1 — Land the right role:** Vivian *is* the decision-maker. Converting her from skeptical scanner to "I need to talk to this person" is the literal mechanism by which the role is landed.
- ✅ **G2 — Make the case undeniable:** Her 30-second scan is the exact test G2 is written against; her positive forces (shipped proof, POV, range) and negative forces (buzzwords, gone-soft, wasted time) define what "undeniable" must mean.
- ✅ **G3 — Be sought, not seeking:** Her "social proof" force and "blind-hire risk" fear are served directly by the reputation/presence work — talks, writing, and recognizable connections make Vivian arrive already half-convinced.
