# Find an Idea Worth Sharing
**Archetype:** Pierce the Peer (tertiary — the downstream amplifier)
**Entry:** Someone he trusts shares a specific essay link — he lands *inside* the writing, not on the home page. Curious but guarded, allergic to self-promotion, ready to skim past anything derivative.
**Goal:** Decide the thinking and the person clear his bar, then share the idea and become willing to vouch for Brandon when a peer asks.

## Screens

1. **An essay (deep link, e.g. `/writing/the-truth-about-simplicity-why-clarity-beats-the-buzzword`)**
   He reads it properly, unlike Vivian and Tessa who scan. The bar is the idea and the voice — a plainly-said point he wishes he'd written. The essays clear it: sharp, unhyped, practitioner-grade. He follows it back to see whether there's more.

2. **Writing index (`/writing`)**
   Work arranged by what it argues (Leadership / Experience Design / AI), "Quarterly. Long-form. No newsletter to sign up for." That last line disarms him — someone who writes to think, not to funnel. The structure reads as a body of work, not content marketing. He registers the depth.

3. **About → Colophon (`/about`)**
   Before he'll vouch — which puts his own name on the line — he checks the person. The "Not doing: newsletter, podcast, personal brand — the work is the brand" block converts respect-for-the-idea into respect-for-the-person. The Colophon reinforces it: a hand-built "how this was made" page is itself an unhyped, practitioner move.

4. **Shares, remembers, ready to vouch** ✓ Success
   He shares the essay (signaling his own taste), files the name, and — the real prize — is ready to give a specific, confident endorsement when a Vivian or Tessa asks around. The loop closes: writing → reader → sharer → voucher. G3 made durable.
