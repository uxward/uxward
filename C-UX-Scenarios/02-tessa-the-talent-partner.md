# Grab a Packageable Pitch, Fast
**Archetype:** Tessa the Talent Partner (secondary — the amplifier)
**Entry:** Active sourcing — a name surfaces from LinkedIn, a referral, or a colleague drops her the link. She arrives brisk and transactional, qualifying in minutes, asking on every glance: *Can I substantiate this?* and *Will this make me look smart or careless?*
**Goal:** Decide fast that Brandon is packageable and verifiable, then forward a clean pitch to a hiring VP without it blowing back on her.

## Screens

1. **Home (`/`)**
   The fold gives her a forwardable summary in one scan — title, "25 years standing up design practice," CXO, "considering senior design leadership · Dallas." She doesn't need proof-in-30-seconds the way Vivian does; she needs legible labels. Grabs the headline and goes hunting for named-company backing.

2. **Work index (`/work`)**
   Her best screen: the wider-archive grid of recognizable enterprise names — a global automaker, Deloitte, NOKIA, Neiman Marcus, American Heart Association, YPO — each a one-line outcome she can quote. Packageable, repeatable, verifiable proof a non-expert can stand behind.

3. **Contact (`/contact`)**
   The FAQ confirms fit before she spends an intro: roles (Head/VP/CDO), location, terms, "no take-home." The explicit, senior ask kills her two biggest risks — forwarding a flameout, and the high-maintenance/unclear candidate. A clear candidate is an easy forward.

4. **Packages and forwards** ✓ Success
   With the title (Home), named-company proof (Work), and confirmed ask (Contact), she writes the intro and forwards the link — Brandon enters a room he wasn't in. G3, "be sought, not seeking," made literal.
